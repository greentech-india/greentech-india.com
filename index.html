The Urban Water Crisis

A city that drowns in July
and thirsts by March.

Every monsoon, Mumbai receives 2,400mm of rainfall — more than enough to sustain the city year-round. Yet by March, bore wells run dry and water tankers queue at housing societies. The reason is not a shortage of rain. It is what happens to that rain the moment it lands on the city. Roads, compacted open spaces, and impervious surfaces shed rainwater directly to drains and the sea — within hours of a shower. The aquifer beneath the city receives almost nothing in return. Mumbai does not have a rainfall problem. It has a recharge problem — and that is a problem engineering can solve.

The BHUMIBLOC® System

Engineered from the sub-grade up.
Not just through the surface.

"Conventional permeable paving allows water through the surface layer. What happens below determines whether recharge actually occurs."

In most urban installations, sub-soil beneath permeable paving is compacted construction fill. Water infiltrates the surface, stalls, and moves laterally into drains — not vertically into the aquifer. The surface looks permeable. The recharge does not happen.

BHUMIBLOC® is a multi-layer engineered sub-base system — depth designed per project and ground conditions. The surface panel is only the beginning. The system is designed from the sub-grade upward to move water to the water table.

The compaction problem: Open grounds, racecourses, parks and institutional campuses are presumed to recharge groundwater naturally. They do not. Years of footfall, vehicle movement and monsoon cycling compact the sub-surface soil to near-impermeability. Water sits on the surface, stagnates, and eventually runs off — contributing nothing to the aquifer below.

Bioswale and water body integration: Bioswales and water bodies are an excellent design intent — they intercept runoff at the surface and slow it down. But without an engineered sub-base below, that water stalls in compacted fill and drains laterally rather than vertically. BHUMIBLOC® transforms a bioswale or water body from a surface retention feature into an active groundwater recharge engine.

The mosquito solution: BHUMIBLOC® moves water downward within hours — eliminating standing water, eliminating the breeding cycle, eliminating the Breach Candy risk.

The Science of Failure

Sponge parks that stop working.
And the solution that does not.

Global research confirms what Mumbai's climate will accelerate: soil-based infiltration systems fail. The park stays green. The recharge stops. BHUMIBLOC® is engineered to be permanent — because its core is inorganic, cannot compact, and has no biological degradation mechanism.

The Science — Peer Reviewed

"Urban green space soil comprehensive performance decays rapidly within 4–6 years. After about 10 years of use, its performance can no longer meet the requirements for sponge city construction." — ScienceDirect, peer-reviewed study on sponge city soils

"Construction activity or compaction treatments reduced infiltration rates 70 to 99 percent in studied urban soils." — Journal of Soil and Water Conservation

China — ₹80 Crore. Still flooded.

Zhengzhou invested over $80 million to become a sponge city. When extreme rain hit in July 2021, the soil saturated immediately. Direct losses exceeded US$10 billion. Over 290 lives lost. In a designated sponge city. Soil-based systems cannot handle Mumbai-scale monsoon events.

Mumbai amplifies every failure.

High water table, reclaimed land, intense monsoon, heavy footfall — Mumbai's conditions degrade soil-based infiltration faster than any temperate climate. A system that fails in 6 years in China may fail in 3 in Mumbai. The engineering must account for this reality.

BHUMIBLOC® — no degradation mechanism.

The structural panel cannot compact. The aggregate interlocks permanently. BHUMIABSORB™ is an inorganic engineered matrix — no biological activity, no compaction, no failure pathway. It performs on Day 1 and on Day 7,300. The 20-year service life is not a target. It is a consequence of the materials science. Proprietary composition — independently tested and certified.

Common Questions

What Mumbai residents
most need to know.

Mumbai receives 2,400mm of rain every year. Why does the city still face a water crisis?
+
Because almost none of that rain reaches the ground. Mumbai's built surface sheds rainwater the moment it falls — off roads, rooftops, and compacted parks into drains and the Arabian Sea within hours of each shower. The aquifer beneath the city receives almost no replenishment. Meanwhile, bore wells extract year-round. The result is a city that drowns in July and thirsts by March. The crisis is not a rainfall problem. It is a recharge problem.
We have a bore well on our property. Is our groundwater supply secure?
+
Not indefinitely — and the situation is worsening. Mumbai has experienced steep, documented drops in groundwater levels. Fire temples in South Mumbai reported failing wells. Housing societies in Mumbai's Central and Northern suburbs report pump run times increasing significantly each year. There are approximately 6,000 active construction sites in Greater Mumbai extracting groundwater as a byproduct. A bore well extracts. It does not recharge. Without active recharge at the surface, every bore well is drawing down a finite resource.
What is the difference between rainwater harvesting and groundwater recharge?
+
Rainwater harvesting collects rain and stores it in a tank for reuse — flushing, gardening, non-potable use. The water stays above ground. Useful — but it does not restore the aquifer. Groundwater recharge returns water to the aquifer — the geological formation from which bore wells draw. For recharge to happen, water must actually infiltrate through the soil and reach the water table. BHUMIBLOC® is a groundwater recharge system. Not a harvesting system.
How does BHUMIBLOC® work differently from conventional permeable paving?
+
Conventional permeable paving allows water through the surface. In most urban installations, the sub-soil below is compacted construction fill. Water infiltrates the surface, stalls, and moves laterally into drains — not vertically into the aquifer. The surface looks permeable. The recharge does not happen. BHUMIBLOC® is a multi-layer engineered sub-base system, depth designed per project. It is designed from the sub-grade upward to move water to the water table. BHUMIABSORB™ — the core module — has confirmed water absorption of 664.12% and is classified Highly Permeable under independent ASTM testing.
Will BHUMIBLOC® work in Mumbai's specific soil conditions?
+
Yes — and Mumbai's conditions are precisely why it is needed. BHUMIBLOC® does not rely on native soil porosity for recharge. The engineered sub-base creates its own detention and release pathway. BHUMIABSORB™ absorbs water at 664.12% of its own weight and releases it progressively to the sub-grade — independent of the native soil's permeability. Where sub-grade is insufficient, the system integrates with vertical recharge bore infrastructure, directing detained water directly to the aquifer — bypassing poor soil entirely.
Is the system depth adequate for Mumbai's monsoon?
+
Yes — and conservatively over-specified for Mumbai's standard design storm. BHUMIABSORB™ holds a very high volume of water per square metre before releasing — combined with the gravel detention layer above it, the total detention capacity exceeds the volume delivered by Mumbai's standard design storm before any sub-grade release is needed. The system is engineered to handle Mumbai's monsoon intensity with a built-in safety margin.
How long does BHUMIBLOC® last? How is 20 years proven?
+
The 20-year service life is proven component by component. The structural surface panel has been manufactured since 2001 — over 24 years of documented global installations still in service. BHUMIABSORB™ is inorganic — it cannot biodegrade, compact, rot, or oxidise. Its NABL-certified performance properties are physical characteristics of the material, not time-dependent. Aggregate has an effectively infinite service life. A structural engineer's professional service life opinion is provided as part of the project commissioning package.
What can India learn from global water infrastructure failures — and how does BHUMIBLOC® avoid them?
+
The world has produced some expensive lessons that India must not repeat. Australia invested billions in seawater desalination — only to discover that the concentrated brine discharged back into the ocean was destroying coral reefs and marine ecosystems. China's multi-billion dollar sponge city programme delivered parks that looked green and performed well for a few years — then silently stopped working as soil compacted and permeability collapsed. The lesson in both cases: solutions that work against nature's systems create new problems while solving old ones. BHUMIBLOC® works with the hydrological cycle — it simply returns the monsoon rain to where it came from: the aquifer below. No brine. No compaction. No downstream consequence. India has the opportunity to lead by example — deploying solutions that are ecologically sound, permanently functional, and designed for Indian conditions from the start.
Is BHUMIBLOC® only for large open land projects?
+
No. The system is modular and scalable — from a 200 square metre housing society garden to a 200,000 square metre open ground. Build-up and performance are identical at every scale. Any permeable open surface — a school playground, a temple courtyard, a housing society garden, a municipal park, a coastal open space — can be converted into an active recharge system. Every surface counts. Mumbai receives enough rain. The only question is whether it reaches the ground.
